-- Game of Life sequencer --

This is a step sequencer that uses John Conway's Game of Life to generate evolving patterns.
The first four rows are a sawtooth synth, the middle four are drum samples and the bottom four are a sine synth.
The next cell generation is produced every 4 steps.
Clicking on cells will toggle their state, allowing to make live changes to the sequence.

At first glance it might sound pretty random, but with some tweaking and playing around with the patterns I find it interesting to listen to the "natural" developments.
For example, sometimes periods of calmness or little percussion will start. Other times the whole "song" will die out on its own.
Small changes with the mouse might introduce a gradual change of melody/beat where you can still hear the existing patterns slowly changing with every new bar, etc.

The sequencer was made with the Tone.js framework, also using Tone.js UI components and some audio samples from their examples.
Source code is available on GitHub.
